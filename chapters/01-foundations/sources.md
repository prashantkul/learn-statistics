# Chapter 1 — Sources

*Foundations: Sample Spaces, Counting, Probability Axioms, Conditional Probability*

## Blitzstein & Hwang, *Introduction to Probability* (2e)

- **Chapter 1: Probability and counting** — pp. 1–44. The intuitive foundation: sample spaces (*Pebble World*), events, naive probability, counting rules (multiplication, permutations, combinations), binomial coefficient identities, and story proofs.
  - §1.2 Sample spaces and Pebble World (pp. 3–5) — the central picture.
  - §1.3 Naive definition of probability (pp. 5–7).
  - §1.4.1 Multiplication rule (p. 8), §1.4.2 Sampling (pp. 10–13).
  - §1.4.3 Adjusting for overcounting / §1.4.4 Story proofs (pp. 13–19).
  - §1.5 Axioms of probability and non-naive definition (pp. 20–22).
  - §1.6–§1.8 Inclusion-exclusion, examples, and R demo (pp. 22–44).
- **Chapter 2: Conditional probability** — pp. 45–102. The operational core of Ch 1: definition, Bayes' rule, law of total probability, independence, conditional independence, coherency, the Monty Hall problem, and Simpson's paradox.
  - §2.1–§2.2 Conditional probability definition (pp. 45–50).
  - §2.3 Bayes' rule + law of total probability (pp. 51–58).
  - §2.4 Conditional probabilities are probabilities (pp. 59–63).
  - §2.5 Independence of events (pp. 64–67).
  - §2.6 Coherency of Bayes' rule (pp. 67–70).
  - §2.7 Conditioning as problem-solving strategy (pp. 70–76) — Monty Hall, gambler's ruin intro.
  - §2.8 Pitfalls — Simpson's paradox (pp. 76–82).

## Wasserman, *All of Statistics*

- **Chapter 1: Probability** — pp. 3–18. Compact re-derivation of the same material: sample spaces, σ-algebras (briefly), probability axioms, independence, conditional probability, Bayes' theorem, Bernoulli and Poisson processes.
  - §1.1–§1.3 Intro, sample spaces, probability (pp. 3–7).
  - §1.4–§1.5 Probability on finite sample spaces, independence (pp. 7–10).
  - §1.6–§1.7 Conditional probability, Bayes' theorem (pp. 10–13).
  - §1.8–§1.9 Bernoulli, Poisson processes (pp. 13–16).
